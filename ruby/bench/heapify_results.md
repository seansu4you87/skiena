So the Naive method of building a heap (starting with an empty heap and inserting all n elements) ended up being considerably faster than Heapify (bubbling down elements in a smart way).  NaiveSorted and NaiveRandom performed nearly linearly, and much faster than HeapifySorted and HeapifyRandom.  However, NaiveReverse performed way worse than HeapifyReverse (NaiveReverse took over 10x NaiveRandom, which HeapifyReverse was barely worse than HeapifyRandom).
